AI agents are systems that do more than generate text. They can plan, call tools, inspect results, and take follow-up actions based on feedback from the environment. A useful agent loop often includes goal interpretation, task decomposition, tool selection, action execution, observation, and revision. This makes agents suitable for coding assistance, research workflows, customer support orchestration, and business process automation.

Tool use is important because a model's internal knowledge is limited and can become outdated. By calling a calculator, a search system, a database, or a local file reader, the agent can work with fresh information and external state. Strong agent design also requires guardrails. The system should limit risky actions, log what the agent did, and require confirmation for high-impact operations. Reliable agents are usually built around narrow responsibilities rather than unlimited autonomy.
