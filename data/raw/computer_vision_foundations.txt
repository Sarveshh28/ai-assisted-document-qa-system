Computer vision focuses on enabling machines to interpret images and video. Classical computer vision relied on hand-engineered features, while modern systems often use convolutional neural networks or vision transformers. Applications include object detection, segmentation, medical imaging, surveillance, and autonomous driving.

Vision transformers adapt the transformer idea to image patches. They model image regions as token-like inputs and learn global relationships across the visual field. This made transformer-based learning a major theme across both language and vision research.
