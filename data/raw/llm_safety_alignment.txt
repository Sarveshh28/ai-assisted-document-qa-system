Large language model safety focuses on making model behavior helpful, honest, and less harmful. Alignment techniques try to shape outputs so the system follows intended values and constraints. Common approaches include supervised instruction tuning, reinforcement learning from human feedback, constitutional prompting, and policy-based refusal behavior for unsafe requests.

Safety work is not only about refusal. It also includes reducing hallucinations, preventing prompt injection failures, improving factual reliability, and handling ambiguous instructions carefully. Evaluation is a major part of alignment because a system can appear safe in simple tests while failing in adversarial settings. Teams often combine automated red teaming, human review, policy checks, and dataset curation to improve safety over time.
